## The Problem

### 1. Cultural Moments Disappear

When something goes viral, the original context often gets lost. The Arab Spring was born on Twitter, but today you can't reconstruct the original threads. Platforms delete content, accounts get banned, and history gets rewritten.

**Impact:** We lose our cultural memory and can't verify what actually happened.

### 2. AI Content is Invisible

AI-generated videos flood social media, but there's no standard way to know:
- Was this made by AI or a human?
- What camera angles and effects were used?
- How was this actually created?

**Impact:** Trust erodes, and creators can't learn from each other's techniques.

### 3. Creators Don't Own Their Value

Creators fuel the cultural economy but:
- Platforms take 30-50% of revenue
- No equity or ownership in what they build
- Value extraction instead of value sharing

**Impact:** The people who create culture can't build wealth from it.

## The Solution: Three Pillars

### üé¨ Pillar 1: AI Video Vault
**A YouTube for the permanent web**

Every video uploaded includes rich metadata:
- Camera angles, lighting, color grading
- AI provenance (which model, what prompts)
- Effects and post-production details

**Why it matters:**
- Videos stored permanently on Arweave (can never be deleted)
- AI agents can learn from real production techniques
- Creators get "Kulture Points" for richer metadata
- Transparency becomes the standard

**Example:** A filmmaker uploads a Gen-3 Alpha video with shot-by-shot metadata. Other creators can see exactly how each scene was composed, and AI researchers can study cinematic techniques at scale.

---

### üì∞ Pillar 2: Kulture Wire
**The decentralized fact-checker**

When something goes viral, Kulture Wire:
1. Traces it back to the original post/thread
2. Captures the real-time reactions when it happened
3. Archives everything permanently on Arweave
4. Issues an "Origin Verified" badge with timestamp

**Why it matters:**
- Cultural moments can't be erased or rewritten
- Context is preserved forever
- Misinformation can be traced to its source
- History becomes verifiable

**Example:** A meme goes viral. Kulture Wire finds the original tweet from 6 months ago, captures the first 100 replies, and creates a permanent record showing exactly when and how it started.
